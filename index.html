Problem Statement

Some people would like to do their reading, but are physically unable to hold a book or turn a page. Our aim is to create an accessory that removes strain off users with hand or arm impairments, ultimately improving accessibility for physical books.

Results of User Testing

User Study Results

User Study Method: We are making participants mimic specific disabilities or injuries, as well as multi-tasking scenarios detailed in the previous constraint ideas.

Rationale for Method: This method ensures that the users actually test the high-fidelity prototype in a very similar manner as the target demographic before providing an opinion as to the efficacy of the product.

Participants: Participants are put in a scenario or constraint that strongly resembles the disabilities of the target demographic.

Recruiting: We gather participants from the Zachry Engineering Center to see our demonstration and answer questions after using the prototype.

Findings and Insights

After surveying testing participants, participants found using the pedal rather than their hands to turn the page much simpler, encouraging them to continue their assignments and hobbies. Participants said given their restrictions, they would continue use. Participants suggested that the prototype be more stable to support different kinds of books. Overall the product was successful.

Analysis

The consistently high ratings across categories tested (including versatility, likelihood of use, stability, idea rating, and ease of use) indicate that the hands free page turner provides excellent user satisfaction.

1. Versatile: Users found the page turner (having a versatility score of 8.27) beneficial for multiple scenarios, not limited to physical disabilities but also helpful for multitasking and temporary injuries.

2. Likelihood of Use: having a versatility score of 7.54 indicates that users perceive our product as something they are inclined to use frequently or regularly when reading a book.

3. Stability: The stability score of 5.9 is our biggest concern, which is something to be improved upon in future iterations.

4. Idea Rating: A high idea rating of 9.27 suggests that users find the product innovative or novel, and prefer the foot pedals to other alternatives presented in the background research section.

5. Ease of Use: rating of 7.64 reflects the user-friendliness of our product, indicating that it is intuitive and accessible, thereby reducing barriers to adoption.

Overall, these high scores demonstrate that the product not only meets but exceeds user expectations across key metrics

Conclusion & Future Work

In conclusion, the user study clearly illustrates the benefit of the product and its usability for making reading physical books more accessible. However, the user study highlights a clear preference among users for a more stable product. Moving forward, future work should focus on refining the design to ensure utmost stability, perhaps building with sturdier materials. Additionally, a comprehensive cost analysis would greatly benefit users regarding the affordability and scalability of the product. Balancing functionality with cost-effectiveness By addressing these aspects, the hands-free page turner can advance the inclusivity of reading experiences for individuals with diverse needs.
